Beautiful Native Species for Your Ontario Garden

Purple Coneflower (Echinacea purpurea)

Stunning purple blooms attract butterflies and bees. Drought-tolerant and low maintenance.

Perfect for: Full sun gardens

Wild Bergamot (Monarda fistulosa)

Fragrant lavender flowers loved by hummingbirds. Excellent for pollinator gardens.

Perfect for: Pollinator gardens

Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta)

Cheerful yellow flowers bloom all summer. Provides seeds for goldfinches in fall.

Perfect for: Sunny borders

Canada Goldenrod (Solidago canadensis)

Late-season golden blooms provide crucial nectar for migrating monarchs.

Perfect for: Wildflower meadows

Red Osier Dogwood (Cornus sericea)

Striking red stems provide winter interest. Berries feed birds year-round.

Perfect for: Shrub borders

Wild Columbine (Aquilegia canadensis)

Delicate red and yellow flowers attract hummingbirds. Thrives in shade.

Perfect for: Shaded gardens

Why Invasive Species Are Harmful

Invasive species outcompete native plants, disrupting ecosystems that took thousands of years to develop. They reduce biodiversity, harm wildlife, and can cost billions in environmental damage and control efforts.

Invasive species are one of the top threats to biodiversity in Ontario and across Canada.
