Why Choose Native Species?

🦋

Support Wildlife

Native plants have evolved alongside local wildlife. They provide essential food and habitat for pollinators, birds, and beneficial insects that invasive species cannot.

💧

Low Maintenance

Native species are adapted to Ontario's climate and soil conditions. They require less water, fertilizer, and pesticides, saving you time and money.

🌍

Ecosystem Health

Native plants preserve biodiversity and strengthen ecosystem resilience. They prevent soil erosion and improve water quality in local watersheds.

Beautiful Native Species for Your Ontario Garden

Purple Coneflower

Echinacea purpurea

Stunning purple blooms attract butterflies and bees. Drought-tolerant and low maintenance.

Perfect for: Full sun gardens

Wild Bergamot

Monarda fistulosa

Fragrant lavender flowers loved by hummingbirds. Excellent for pollinator gardens.

Perfect for: Pollinator gardens

Black-Eyed Susan

Rudbeckia hirta

Cheerful yellow flowers bloom all summer. Provides seeds for goldfinches in fall.

Perfect for: Sunny borders

Canada Goldenrod

Solidago canadensis

Late-season golden blooms provide crucial nectar for migrating monarchs.

Perfect for: Wildflower meadows

Red Osier Dogwood

Cornus sericea

Striking red stems provide winter interest. Berries feed birds year-round.

Perfect for: Shrub borders

Wild Columbine

Aquilegia canadensis

Delicate red and yellow flowers attract hummingbirds. Thrives in shade.

Perfect for: Shaded gardens

The Invasive Species Problem

⚠️

Why Invasive Species Are Harmful

Invasive species outcompete native plants, disrupting ecosystems that took thousands of years to develop. They reduce biodiversity, harm wildlife, and can cost billions in environmental damage and control efforts.

Invasive species are one of the top threats to biodiversity in Ontario and across Canada.

Common Invasive Species to Avoid

🚫 Purple Loosestrife

Lythrum salicaria

Harmful: Forms dense stands in wetlands, choking out native plants.

✅ Use: Cardinal Flower

🚫 Norway Maple

Acer platanoides

Harmful: Creates dense shade, prevents native understory growth.

✅ Use: Sugar Maple

🚫 Common Buckthorn

Rhamnus cathartica

Harmful: Forms impenetrable thickets, alters soil chemistry.

✅ Use: Nannyberry

🚫 Japanese Knotweed

Fallopia japonica

Harmful: Extremely aggressive, damages infrastructure.

✅ Use: Joe-Pye Weed

🚫 Periwinkle/Vinca

Vinca minor

Harmful: Forms dense mats, smothers native wildflowers.

✅ Use: Wild Ginger

🚫 Burning Bush

Euonymus alatus

Harmful: Escapes cultivation, invades forests.

✅ Use: Maple-leaved Viburnum
